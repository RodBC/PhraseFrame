          THE EVIDENCE, WITHOUT THE HYPE

          Understanding compounds when focus, checking, and spacing work together.

          01

          Focus

          Phrase-RSVP reduces scanning so attention stays on meaning—not on hunting the next line.

          02

          Pace

          Deep work often lives near 250–350 WPM. Adults average ~238 WPM on nonfiction; we warn you as you stretch past ~350.

          03

          Check

          Brief tests during learning cut mind-wandering probes roughly in half in landmark lecture research (~19% vs ~40%)—transfer to reading is plausible, not guaranteed.

          04

          Repair

          When a stop is weak, you get a short extractive recap and a gap list—so repair is specific, not a blind reread.

          05

          Keep

          Recall practice beat rereading at one week in classic studies (61% vs 40% idea units). Spaced review can add large gains vs cramming for the same study time.

          What the research actually says

          RSVP can calm the eyes and enforce a pace, but it does not delete the time language needs. Regressions, pauses, and slower speeds remain tools—not failures.

          What we lean on instead of speed myths:

          • Practice testing and distributed practice — high utility (Dunlosky et al., 2013)
          • Testing vs rereading — 61% vs 40% idea-unit recall at 1 week (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006)
          • Interpolated tests — mind-wandering ~19% vs ~40% of probes in online lectures (Szpunar et al., 2013)
          • RSVP comprehension — often similar to page reading at 250–350 WPM; weaker at 400–450 (Ricciardi, 2018)

          We do not promise 2–3× speed with full comprehension, silent “subvocalization hacks,” or medical treatment of attention disorders. PhraseFrame has no controlled outcome study—literature numbers are tagged separately from your in-app self-check scores.
