# Scientific position

PhraseFrame uses rapid serial visual presentation (RSVP) with short phrase segments. The
interface can reduce visual scanning and enforce a steady pace, but it cannot remove the time
needed to understand and remember language.

## Product claims we can support

- Structured short segments can be more readable than arbitrary segments.
- A fixed focal location reduces the need to scan lines.
- Readers need control over speed, pauses, and regressions.
- Faster presentation creates a comprehension trade-off, especially for difficult material.

We must not claim guaranteed comprehension, attention treatment, or effortless reading at
500–1,000 WPM. A 2018 study with 209 participants found no significant comprehension difference
between traditional reading and RSVP at 250–350 WPM, but poorer results at 400–450 WPM. Other
work finds costs beginning at lower rates, so the UI treats 350 WPM as guidance—not a biological
threshold.
